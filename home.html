Introduction

The program is a rudimentary physics engine that approximately simulates the motion of a rocket under the gravitational influence of planets that are placed by the user. The user inputs the positions of the planets, choosing from five different possible masses, and inputs the initial velocity of the rocket.

How does it work?

The program stores the rocket's position and velocity, updating these values periodically to numerically continue the rocket's trajectory. At the end of each time interval, the program calculates the net force on the rocket in each direction by applying Newton's Law of Gravitation,

\[F_x=-\sum_{p\in\text{PlanetList}}\frac{Gm_p m_{\text{rocket}}}{r_{p,\text{rocket}}^3}(x_{\text{rocket}}-x_p)\]

\[F_y=-\sum_{p\in\text{PlanetList}}\frac{Gm_p m_{\text{rocket}}}{r_{p,\text{rocket}}^3}(y_{\text{rocket}}-y_p)\]

We apply Newton's second law of motion to then obtain the accelerations at that instant.

\[a_x=\frac{F_x}{m_{rocket}}\]

\[a_y=\frac{F_y}{m_{rocket}}\]

Then we update the position of the rocket and then the velociy.

\[x=x+v_x\Delta t\]

\[y=y+v_y\Delta t\]

\[v_x=v_x+a_x\Delta t\]

\[v_y=v_y+a_y\Delta t\]

By repeating this at the end of each time interval, we approximately calculate the rockets trajectory.
